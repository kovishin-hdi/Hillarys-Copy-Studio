Underneath both — the money, as the customer was told it

From the leaflet, now canonical in the Charter. Three payments, each doing one specific thing — and copy must never blur them.

Token · Price freeze
5% / ₹5,000

Whichever is higher. Freezes the price version and agreed discount. Does not process the order or start procurement.

Total advance
50%

Processes the order, schedules check-measurement, starts procurement. No changes, cancellation, or refund after this point.

Balance
50%

Payable when products are ready for dispatch. Then installation is scheduled at the customer’s convenience.

The craft

Anatomy of a lifecycle message

A bare status update is a ticket system — the thing this brand exists to replace. Every message carries three payloads, in order, plus a named owner.

Quick update {name} — fabric stitching is complete and your order is now in quality check.

Every seam, fold, and finish is being checked against our standards before it leaves us — nothing reaches your home until it’s right.

*Here are the NEXT STEPS:*
1. Once it clears QC, we share your balance and payment link.
2. On payment, we dispatch your order and arrange installation.

1 · The statusWhat just happened, in plain, calm language. The fact the trigger exists to report.
2 · The craft lineOne sentence that reframes logistics as handling and care. It’s what makes an operational update feel premium.
3 · The two-step lookaheadThe anti-silence mechanism: the customer always knows what comes next, so uncertainty never has room to grow.
Always · A named ownerThe message comes from a person — the CRM set up in T1 — never from “our team.” The leaflet promised this in writing.
Layer 3 · The fact base

The Order Journey Charter

Sourced from the leaflet in every customer’s hand. These are the facts no draft may contradict — because the customer can point at the paper and say “but you told me…”

The quote is indicative

~90–95% accurate at consultation, valid 15 days. Final price is confirmed only after the check-measurement visit.

The real timeline

15 working days from check-measurement — not calendar days from order. Longer for motorised or imported fabric. This is why marketing copy never promises “15-day delivery.”

Procurement is split

Curtain and Roman-blind fabric is ordered the same day as the 50%. Other blinds, hardware, and components only after check-measurement confirms final specs.

Installation rules

One visit is included; a re-visit is chargeable if the site isn’t ready. The customer inspects and signs the acknowledgement at installation.

After-care window

Defects or damage reported within 48 hours, with photos, to orders@hillarys.co.in. Warranty covers manufacturing defects only.

Who to reach

Level 1: Design Consultant / CSR / Installation Manager by stage. Level 2: ASM / Sales Manager. Level 3: Customer Service — the escalation ladder every recovery message routes along.

We’ll keep you informed at every stage — you shouldn’t have to chase us for status updates. Every person who visits your home will be introduced to you in advance, by name. — The Commitment, from the customer-facing leaflet. The anti-silence mandate and the ownership rule, promised on paper.
Governance

What it enforces, what it refuses

The studio is allowed to push back — that is the job. Every draft runs the rejection list before it ships.

Enforced on every draft

  • The handling test — does this make “we handle this so you don’t have to” more true or less true?
  • Ownership visibility — a named person and a clear next step, drawn from the Charter’s roster.
  • Fact consistency — every %, timeline, owner, and rule matches the leaflet.
  • Voice by stage — warm at attention, transparent through the wait, celebratory at handover, grateful after.
  • Calm over hype — no urgency, scarcity, or discount pressure, ever.

Refused outright

  • “15-day delivery” in marketing — the true fact is 15 working days from check-measurement; say “about three weeks” or stay silent.
  • “Premium London-based brand” — replaced with “50 years of European craftsmanship, now made in India.”
  • Discount-led campaigns and loud-luxury language.
  • “Refer and earn ₹X” — we invite people to share the experience, with a token of appreciation.
  • Unowned messages — if no one stands behind it by name, it doesn’t send.
The flywheel, turning

Open decisions — v1.3.0

The Charter surfaced four gaps between the leaflet and the current libraries. They’re flagged for marketing and operations, not silently patched — closing them is the next turn of the loop.

  1. Trigger 1 over-claims procurement. It says all fabrics are procured today; the leaflet splits procurement, and the real rule is broader — no changes at all after the 50%. Revise T1.
  2. The token freeze is a silent step. The customer’s first payment (5% / ₹5,000) has no WhatsApp message. Recommend a price_frozen trigger in the standard anatomy.
  3. Two support numbers coexist. The leaflet’s 090082 81888 and the CRM toll-free +91-7795830298. Confirm which is canonical for which purpose.
  4. One public timeline phrasing. Agree the approved wording — e.g. “made to measure in about three weeks” — so sales, lifecycle, and marketing all say the same true thing.
